Phishing is a type of cyberattack in which attackers try to deceive individuals into sharing sensitive information, like passwords, credit card numbers, or other personal data. The attacker often poses as a legitimate organization or person to trick the target into revealing confidential information.

The purpose of phishing is typically to gain unauthorized access to accounts, commit identity theft, or steal financial information. Attackers may use this information directly for fraudulent activities or sell it on the dark web to other criminals.

The name "phishing" indeed comes from the concept of "fishing." Just as a fisherman uses bait to catch fish, cybercriminals use fake messages or websites to "hook" victims into giving away valuable information. The term was first used in the mid-1990s by hackers who were "fishing" for passwords and financial data. The ph spelling is believed to be an homage to early hackers who used similar alternative spellings (like in phreaking for phone hacking).

History and evolution of phishing techniques.

Phishing has a fascinating history that dates back to the early days of the internet and has evolved significantly over the years. Here’s an overview of its origins and how it has transformed:

The use of digital communications to trick people into revealing sensitive data or deploying malicious software.

Some of the most common types of phishing attacks today include:

  • Business Email Compromise (BEC): A threat actor sends an email message that seems to be from a known source to make seemingly legitimate requests for information, in order to obtain a financial advantage.
  • Spear Phishing: A malicious email attack that targets a specific user or group of users. The email seems to originate from a trusted source.
  • Whaling: A form of spear phishing. Threat actors target company executives to gain access to sensitive data.
  • Vishing: The exploitation of electronic voice communication to obtain sensitive information or to impersonate a known source.
  • Smishing: The use of text messages to trick users, in order to obtain sensitive information or to impersonate a known source.
